## Abstract

This repository documents solar geometry, spectral science, and shadow analysis in relation to Codex Atlanticus f.673r (CA673) and the Lion Rooms of the Palazzo Vecchio, Florence. The study tests the solar bearing alignment for the feast of Saint John the Baptist (24 June 1518, Julian calendar), including atmospheric refraction modelling and optical geometry. A 156.4° anomaly in one test bearing remains unresolved and is flagged as an open hypothesis requiring further analysis.

---

## Evidence and Caution Statement

Solar bearing test data is deposited in verifiable CSV and KML format. Findings are **Tier B** (strongly supported by geometric evidence) unless otherwise noted. The 156.4° anomaly is explicitly flagged **unresolved** — it is not presented as confirmation of any hypothesis. The full spectral analysis paper (`.docx`) represents working research, not a finalised publication.
