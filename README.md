## Key Finding: The 156.4° Anomaly

The direct tower-shadow bearing required to hit the western Lion-room position is ~156.4°. On 24 June 1518 Julian (feast of Saint John the Baptist), the maximum achievable evening shadow bearing from the Palazzo Vecchio tower is approximately 122.5°. This creates an unresolved geometric discrepancy of ~34°.

**Status: Unresolved anomaly.** The direct tower-shadow hypothesis does not achieve the required bearing on this date. Three candidate resolutions are documented in the primary paper: (1) alternate mirror/optical system; (2) alternate Lion-room location; (3) alternate date.

The near-noon street-axis lock (shadow bearing ~10.28° at ~12:16 local solar) is confirmed as geometrically achievable and corresponds closely to the Via dei Leoni northward axis.
